Who are we?

Hack4Impact Chapter
Emory H4I is a chapter of Hack4Impact. help nonprofits do the good they are meant to be doing more efficiently. By building them the custom software they need, they can focus on their important work.
Students
All members are a diverse group of active students of Emory University.
Developers
We are students who have/are studying computer science, who have done internships, or are actively developing software.
Designers
We are students who have/are studying UI/UX, have done internships, or actively designing software.
Leaders
We are students passionate about social good and want to support you!
Teachers
While committed to nonprofits, Emory H4I is also committed to providing fruitful experiences to students who can apply their skills anywhere.

Do you have hidden fees?

There are no development fees charged, but you might expect costs for technology depending on your requirements.

How long will it take to work with Emory H4I?

We try our best to schedule projects to last no longer than an academic semester, but depending on project length, it may go over.

Who maintains the solution?

Emory H4I offers to maintain the solution we develop for you, but we are always able to transfer ownership of any software written.

Who is developing?

Emory H4I developers are current Emory University Students who are knowledgeable or have been trained in various skills.

Do I need a well-thoughout solution prepared?

No! If you have any idea, but not quite sure how to get there, feel free to contact us. We are happy to help consult your idea and see how Emory H4I can help!

How can I contact you?

Please reach out to our email at emory@hack4impact.org
